PROJECT 01
IEEE × Keebs Macropad
IEEE at UCI & Keebs Club
Winter 2025–2026
KiCad PCB Design Soldering QMK Firmware Electronics Schematic Design
Participated in a collaborative multi-session workshop series organized by IEEE at UCI and the Keebs Club to design, assemble, and flash a fully functional 3×3 mechanical macropad from the ground up. The project spanned PCB design in KiCad, 3D-printed enclosure design, hand soldering, and QMK firmware flashing.
PCB Design — KiCad
  • Designed a complete schematic in KiCad including a 3×3 key switch matrix with diode array to prevent ghosting, an Arduino Pro Micro microcontroller with proper VCC/GND power rails, an I²C OLED display connector (SDA/SCL), and a reset circuit (10kΩ pull-up resistor + tactile switch).
  • Learned key PCB design concepts: component footprint assignment (MX-NoLED Cherry switches, D3 THT diodes, ProMicro v3), trace routing across copper layers (F.Cu and B.Cu), via placement to switch layers, and copper flood fills for a common ground plane.
  • Performed footprint-to-schematic annotation and transferred the design to a PCB layout with precise component placement matching the physical 3D enclosure geometry (80×58mm board outline).
Soldering & Assembly
  • Soldered a through-hole diode array, resistor, Arduino Pro Micro pin headers, and Cherry MX key switches onto the bare PCB with proper technique — tinned tip, 325°C iron, flux management, and lead clipping.
  • Assembled the complete macropad: inserted switches into the 3D-printed plate, oriented the plate with the PCB, attached keycaps, and reinforced the USB connector with hot glue to prevent mechanical stress failure.
  • Flashed QMK open-source keyboard firmware to the Pro Micro to map key functions and enable custom layers.
Key Skills Demonstrated

End-to-end PCB development from schematic capture through physical assembly and firmware flashing. Hands-on proficiency with soldering tools and workflow, practical understanding of switch matrix logic, diode ghosting prevention, and microcontroller integration — skills directly applicable to any electronics design or hardware engineering role.

PROJECT 02
UAVs at UCI - Tinywhoop Drone
Unmanned Aerial Vehicles
at UCI
September 2025 – Present
Betaflight PID Tuning Soldering ESC / Flight Controller FPV Systems
As a member of the UAVs at UCI Tinywhoop team, built and configured a BETAFPV Air65 micro racing drone from components. The Air65 is a sub-65mm class FPV platform featuring a 5-in-1 flight controller (FC + 4-in-1 ESC + OSD + VTX + RX), 0702SE II 27,000KV brushless motors, and a 160° FOV C03 FPV camera.
Assembly & Configuration
  • Assembled the BETAFPV Air65 by integrating all electronic components into the micro frame: soldered motor leads to the 5-in-1 flight controller board, connected the FPV camera, and secured the canopy.
  • Programmed and configured the flight controller using Betaflight software, adjusting PID (Proportional, Integral, Derivative) tuning parameters to optimize flight dynamics and motor response for racing.
  • Developed practical understanding of the full UAV electronics stack: IMU (gyro/accelerometer), barometer, OSD overlay, power BEC regulation, and radio receiver protocols (ExpressLRS).
  • Applied motor KV theory to understand the trade-off between speed and torque — 27,000KV motors selected for racing-optimized sharp, snappy response.
Key Skills Demonstrated

Hands-on integration of a complete embedded electronics stack — soldering, firmware configuration, PID control theory, and power system understanding across motors, ESCs, and regulation circuits. Practical exposure to radio communication protocols and real-time control systems tuning in a high-iteration, build-test-tune engineering cycle.

PROJECT 03
Honda Automotive Projects
Personal Projects
1997 Honda Accord (V6)
1999 Honda Prelude (H22A4)
Hondata S300 ECU AFR / VTEC Tuning Hydraulic Systems Engine Swap Planning SOLIDWORKS AEM ECU
Two ongoing personal automotive projects involving hands-on mechanical repair, aftermarket ECU installation, engine tuning, and a planned engine swap with custom fabricated components — driven entirely by self-directed curiosity and a desire to understand internal combustion powertrain systems at a deep, hands-on level.
1999 Honda Prelude — H22A4 Engine Tuning
  • Diagnosed and replaced a failed hydraulic clutch master cylinder and slave cylinder — restoring clutch hydraulic pressure and drivability.
  • Installed a Hondata S300 engine management module on the H22A4 DOHC VTEC engine, enabling full ECU tunability on an otherwise locked stock Honda ECU.
  • Adjusted air-fuel ratios (AFR) across the RPM range and optimized the VTEC cam-profile engagement point for maximum power output while maintaining drivability.
1997 Honda Accord — J35A4 Engine Swap (In Progress)
  • Researched and planned a C27A4 V6-to-J35A4 engine swap, selecting a J35A4 block with J32A2 Type-S heads, cams, and intake manifold to create a high-compression, high-flow "Frankenstein" configuration capable of 300+ whp with boost.
  • Currently modeling custom engine mount brackets in SOLIDWORKS to adapt J-series engine geometry to the Accord's C27-spec engine bay — a fabrication challenge requiring precision CAD and understanding of structural load paths.
  • Researching AEM V2 standalone ECU integration for full tunability with a forced induction setup, including volumetric efficiency table mapping for a custom tune.
Key Skills Demonstrated

Real-world mechanical and powertrain engineering spanning hydraulic systems diagnosis, ECU installation and calibration, engine parameter mapping (AFR, VTEC), and custom fabrication planning via SOLIDWORKS CAD. The engine swap project in particular demonstrates the ability to research complex engineering challenges independently, synthesize technical constraints, and execute a multi-stage fabrication plan.

PROJECT 04
FRC Robot — Atlas
FRC Team 2854
"The Prototypes"
2021 – 2025 · Crescendo 2024
Electrical Lead Power Distribution CAN Bus / Ethernet Computer Vision Autonomous Systems Wiring Schematics
Served as Electrical Lead for FRC Team 2854 "The Prototypes" across four seasons, culminating in the 2024 Crescendo competition robot Atlas — a machine capable of collecting foam rings from the ground and source, shooting them into a raised Speaker using a pivoting shooter, and climbing chains at the Stage endgame structure. The team qualified for the FIRST Championship.
Electrical Systems Design
  • Engineered the complete power distribution network and CAN/Ethernet communication bus for all onboard motors, sensors, and compute devices. Created detailed wiring schematics to streamline integration and debugging.
  • Implemented a real-time computer vision system incorporating AprilTag-based field localization and neural network object detection to execute precise automated actions — including ground ring pickup and projectile delivery from the pivoting shooter — contributing to multi-note autonomous routines (2, 3, 4, and 5 note autos).
  • Contributed over 40,000 lines of code across four award-winning competition robots, developing and testing complex autonomous routines using a simulated physics environment.
  • Led hands-on workshops and delegated sub-team tasks, mentoring new members in precision wiring, crimping, and electromechanical design.
Key Skills Demonstrated

Full ownership of an embedded electrical architecture under real competition constraints — including power distribution, CAN bus communication networks, sensor integration, wiring schematic documentation, and real-time autonomous systems. Four years of iteration across award-winning robots demonstrates sustained technical leadership and the ability to ship complex hardware on a deadline.
